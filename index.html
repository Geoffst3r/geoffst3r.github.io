Geoffst3r the Jester

A term of endearment, with some truth to it.

Jester -- a person who habitually plays the fool.
Nobody likes to try to lighten the mood of a difficult situation more than me. That is where this name and endearing term came from. Whether I am at work or conversing, feeling the intensity of a situation can be overwhelming.

Before I began my coding bootcamp at App Academy, many of the situations I came across in the construction industry were tough to handle and left most people feeling tense...an obstacle was discovered when trying to dig a trench, or a product was not easily collectible, or any one of a thousand things could have and did go wrong. Because I was on the construction management side, finding a solution to any problem encountered during construction work was something my company and I needed to do. To approach finding a solution, feeling like the issues were not a big deal was something that I felt was important to portray. Thus, being a jester came in handy.

To find another industry to get into, I thought back to what I enjoyed working on back in college: programming. The first algorithm I came up with was for fractal modeling during my intro to systems engineering class my junior year. Before that, I developed a small block of Arduino code along with my group during the first year engineering projects class to assist in building a forget-me-not, a system that notified users what products they tagged were not on their person when they left their house. At the time, it was not suitable to change my major. However, with appAcademy's 1000hr web developlment bootcamp, I am finally ready to step into this field.

Because I am a jester, I never run from difficult situations, and am able to face more challenging situations because of how I can lighten the mood. Tense and difficult situations are where I thrive. When some people have a lot on their plate, they cave under the pressure, whereas I am able to look at the situation differently and thrive. Finish the challenge fast and well, and enjoy myself afterward. Face it, every team needs a jester.
